Technology that reveals what the building cannot show you yet

Example use case: keeping warm roofs dry

Warm roof systems can trap moisture if water ingress, construction moisture, or vapour movement is not managed properly. Monitoring helps confirm whether the roof is remaining dry over time or whether hidden moisture is building up beneath the membrane.

Why warm roofs need monitoring

  • Moisture can remain concealed within insulation or structural layers.
  • Damage may develop long before leaks are visible internally.
  • Drying performance can be verified after installation or remediation work.
  • Evidence-based monitoring helps reduce unnecessary invasive inspections.

What building teams gain

  • Earlier warning of elevated humidity conditions.
  • Better understanding of condensation risk in changing weather conditions.
  • Data to support warranty, maintenance, and lifecycle decisions.
  • Greater confidence that the roof build-up is performing as intended.

A simple process from install to insight

The goal is not just collecting data. It is turning building humidity information into practical decisions that protect the asset.

Assess the structure

Identify the parts of the building where hidden moisture risk is highest, such as roof zones, penetrations, or vulnerable interfaces.

Install and monitor

Place sensors strategically and track humidity and temperature conditions over time rather than relying on isolated spot checks.

Interpret the data

Review patterns, compare seasonal behaviour, and identify whether the assembly is drying, stable, or at growing risk.

Act with confidence

Use measured evidence to plan inspections, maintenance, or remedial work where it is actually needed.
